Belgium

Belgium is an EU and Schengen country, and the currency is the euro (EUR/€).

The national languages are Dutch, French and German. Leuven is officially a monolingually Dutch-speaking city. Most Belgians have a fairly good command of the English language.

Getting to Leuven

By train

NMBS train planners: domestic trains - international train connections.
In practice Deutsche Bahn may be best aware of train connections anywhere in Europe (without necessarily being able to book them).

Leuven/Louvain railway station has direct connections to:

  • Brussels-Airport Zaventem (not to be confused with Zaventem proper), a railway station directly underneath Belgium's main airport,
  • Brussels-South/Brussel-Zuid/Bruxelles-Midi, with direct high-speed connections to London (MGS 2024 in Leicester), Paris (incl. airport) and various destinations in France,
  • Luik/Liège-Guillemins, with direct high-speed connections to Frankfurt am Main (incl. airport) and Dortmund via Köln/Cologne; 3x/week a night train to Vienna; and direct IC connections to Luxembourg (ETAPS 2024),
  • Antwerp-Central/Antwerpen-Centraal/Anvers-Central, with direct IC and high-speed connections to Rotterdam, Amsterdam and Schiphol Airport; and 3x/week a night train to Berlin.
Note: Railway announcements are made in the language of wherever you are (this even holds within a moving train!), and place names are translated. Note that the French name for Leuven is Louvain, but Louvain-la-Neuve is an entirely different place.

Heverlee railway station is not very well connected and is not that much closer to the campus.

By car

Just drive to Leuven. Note that Belgium has severe traffic congestions, especially towards Brussels and Antwerp in the morning and in opposite direction in the evening.

By plane

Some nearby airports that are easily reachable by train are Brussels-Airport (BRU), Paris Charles-de-Gaulle (CDG), Frankfurt am Main (FRA), Amsterdam Schiphol Airport (AMS).
Note that Brussels-South Charleroi Airport (CRL) is not at all in Brussels and does not have a railway station.

Local transport in Leuven

Bike

By far the easiest mode of local transport in Leuven is the bike, especially since the Arenberg science campuses are located outside of the city center.
  • Velo bike rental near the railway station. (Don't click "Fietsverhuur" (bike rental) but just scroll down for the short-term tariffs.)
  • Blue-bike: shared bikes to pick up at Leuven/Heverlee railway station and drop off at the same location. Cheap yearly subscription + low price per fare. (Not yet sure what's the best way to subscribe if you live abroad.)

Walk

You'll rarely have to walk more than a few kilometers, so it's a serious option if you like walking.

Bus

Public buses in Flanders are served by De Lijn.
Relevant bus stops are Heverlee Celestijnenlaan and Heverlee Campus Arenberg.
The city center's bus hub is Rector De Somerplein (which is spread over Rector De Somerplein, Margarethaplein and Grote Markt).
  • Bus 2 "Heverlee Campus" frequently drives from Leuven railway station to Heverlee Celestijnenlaan & Campus Arenberg (terminus). It takes a big detour.
  • Bus 2 "Kessel-Lo Hulsberg" goes back.
  • Bus 616 is a faster but less frequent connection between Leuven railway station, Heverlee Celestijnenlaan (not Campus Arenberg!) and Brussels Airport (but take a big margin if you're going to take the bus to your plane).
