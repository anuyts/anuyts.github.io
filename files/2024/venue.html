Getting to Leuven

By train

NMBS train planners: domestic trains - international train connections.
In practice Deutsche Bahn may be best aware of train connections anywhere in Europe (without necessarily being able to book them). Leuven/Louvain railway station has direct connections to:
  • Brussels-Airport Zaventem (not to be confused with Zaventem proper), a railway station directly underneath Belgium's main airport,
  • Brussels-South/Brussel-Zuid/Bruxelles-Midi, with direct high-speed connections to London (MGS 2024 in Leicester), Paris (incl. airport) and various destinations in France,
  • Luik/Liège-Guillemins, with direct high-speed connections to Frankfurt am Main (incl. airport) and Dortmund via Köln/Cologne; 3x/week a night train to Vienna; and direct IC connections to Luxembourg (ETAPS 2024),
  • Antwerp-Central/Antwerpen-Centraal/Anvers-Central, with direct IC and high-speed connections to Rotterdam, Amsterdam and Schiphol Airport; and 3x/week a night train to Berlin.
Note: Belgium is a multilingual country; railway announcements are made in the language of wherever you are (this even holds within a moving train!), and place names are translated. Note that the French name of Leuven is Louvain, but Louvain-la-Neuve is an entirely different place.

By car

Just drive to Leuven. Note that Belgium has severe traffic congestions, especially towards Brussels and Antwerp in the morning and in opposite direction in the evening.

By plane

Some nearby airports that are easily reachable by train are Brussels-Airport (BRU), Paris Charles-de-Gaulle (CDG), Frankfurt am Main (FRA), Amsterdam Schiphol Airport (AMS).
Note that Brussels-South Charleroi Airport (CRL) is not at all in Brussels and does not have a railway station.
