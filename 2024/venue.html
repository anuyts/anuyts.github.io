Belgium

Belgium is an EU and Schengen country, and the currency is the euro (EUR/€).

The national languages are Dutch, French and German. Leuven is officially a monolingually Dutch-speaking city. Most Belgians have a fairly good command of the English language.

Emergency number: 112
Country calling code: +32
Electricity: ~230V, power outlet of type C/E (compatible with F) - Wikipedia

Few grocery stores and very few non-grocery stores are open on Sunday. Monday is another popular closing day for non-grocery shops.

Getting to Leuven

By train

NMBS train planners: domestic trains - international train connections.
In practice Deutsche Bahn may be best aware of train connections anywhere in Europe (without necessarily being able to book them).

Leuven/Louvain railway station has direct connections to:

  • Brussels-Airport Zaventem (not to be confused with Zaventem proper), a railway station directly underneath Belgium's main airport,
  • Brussels-South/Brussel-Zuid/Bruxelles-Midi, with direct high-speed connections to London (MGS 2024 in Leicester), Paris (incl. airport) and various destinations in France,
    • Barcelona-Sants can be reached from Brussels-South with a single change in Valence TGV or Lyon Part-Dieu. You have to force planner tools to find this; they will instead recommend that you change in Paris but then you have to hurry through the Parisian metro.
  • Luik/Liège-Guillemins, with direct high-speed connections to Frankfurt am Main (incl. airport) and Dortmund via Köln/Cologne; 3x/week a night train to Vienna; and direct IC connections to Luxembourg (ETAPS 2024),
    • From Köln Hbf, there are direct connections to Hamburg Hbf, where you have:
      • Connections to Denmark,
      • A direct night train to Malmö and Stockholm,
  • Antwerp-Central/Antwerpen-Centraal/Anvers-Central, with direct IC and high-speed connections to Rotterdam, Amsterdam and Schiphol Airport; and 3x/week a night train to Berlin and Prague.
Note: Railway announcements in Belgium are made in the language of wherever you are (this even holds within a moving train!), and place names are translated. Note that the French name for Leuven is Louvain, but Louvain-la-Neuve is an entirely different place.

Heverlee railway station is not very well connected, nor is it very helpful to get to the Arenberg campus.

Local transport in Leuven

Bike

By far the easiest mode of local transport in Leuven is the bike, especially since the Arenberg science campuses are located outside of the city center.

  • Velo bike rental near the railway station. (Don't click "Fietsverhuur" (bike rental) but just scroll down for the short-term tariffs.)
  • Blue-bike: shared bikes to pick up at Leuven/Heverlee railway station and drop off at the same location. Cheap yearly subscription + low price per fare. Subscription is apparently best done well in advance so you can get your membership card delivered by post at your home address, even abroad.

Note: Conflict-free crossroads are the exception in Belgium. So even if you have a green light for cyclists, beware of cars turning left or right, although they should yield.

Walk

You'll rarely have to walk more than a few kilometers, so it's a serious option if you like walking.

Bus

Public buses in Flanders are served by De Lijn.

Tickets can be bought via their app (official versions only iOS and Google Play Store), or at a vending machine or partnered store.

Relevant bus stops are Heverlee Celestijnenlaan (in front of the campus) and Heverlee Campus Arenberg (in front of the CS building).
The city center's bus hub is Rector De Somerplein (which is spread over Rector De Somerplein, Margarethaplein and Grote Markt).

  • Bus 2 "Heverlee Campus" frequently drives from Leuven railway station via Heverlee railway station to Heverlee Celestijnenlaan & Campus Arenberg (terminus in front of the CS building). It takes a significant detour.
  • Bus 2 "Kessel-Lo Hulsberg" goes back (and continues beyond Leuven railway station).
  • Bus 616 (and back) is a faster but less frequent connection between Leuven railway station, Heverlee railway station, Heverlee Celestijnenlaan (not Campus Arenberg!) and Brussels Airport (but take a big margin if you're going to take the bus to your plane).

Car

The car is not an efficient means of transport in the city centre (within the ring road) or in Kessel-Lo. The traffic flow is usually relatively good on the ring road (especially the North-West side), Koning Boudewijnlaan and Celestijnenlaan.
